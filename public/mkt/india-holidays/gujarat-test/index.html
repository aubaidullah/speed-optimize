About Gujarat
The state soaked rich in culture and diversity is an ideal tourist hub. There are plenty of places worth a visit here and needs to be planned well in advance. Some of the best places to visit in Gujarat are the Sabarmati Ashram, the residence of the Father of the Nation that is now converted into a museum; the Statue of Unity built recently on the river Narmada; the Hindu religious touchpoints like the ancient Somnath Temple which finds a mention in the Rigveda, Dwarkadheesh Temple in Dwarka dedicated Lord Krishna ; the Sun Temple at Modhera; Rani Ki Vav, a world heritage site; the Swaminarayan Akshardham temple in Gandhinagar and the Jain temples of Palitana that has more than 900 holy temples. For nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts, a one-day tour of the Gir National Park on a jeep safari to catch a glimpse of the Asiatic Lions, an endangered species is a must. For people who love to unravel the history of a place, a visit to the UNESCO certified monuments at Champaner; a visit to the Laxmi Niwas Palace built by the erstwhile rulers of Baroda; a recce of the Lakhota Museum at Jamnagar to view historic artefacts from the bygone eras; and the climb to the Pavagadh Hills is surely not to be missed. Get a taste of the tribal life-style in the lush green forests of Jambughoda; walk through the incredible work of nature at the Rann of Kutch, one of the world?s flattest surfaces where salt from the sea gets deposited and experience a unique eco stay in rural Dang and acquaint yourself with the rustic life of the natives here. Choose the best Gujarat tour packages so that all the key attractions in the state are part of your itinerary. An event that is worthy of participating here is in the forests of Dang where millions and millions of fireflies meet once a year to search their partners. As a result, the entire forest and the night lights up providing the tourists a memorable experience for a lifetime. Stay at the Satpura hill station, amidst the wilderness on the way back. more... less..
